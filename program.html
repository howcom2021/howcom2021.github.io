Program

Date of the workshop: 11. October 2021 (Monday).

This is a full-day, fully online workshop, with two distinct parts.

All times are GMT.

I. Morning block: Presentations (12:00-14:20)

We will entertain a range of presentations in the form of vision paper presentations and case paper presentations. Each talk is 15 minutes long, and is followed by 5 minutes of Q&A. Talks will be recorded and published on the workshop website.

  • 12:35-12:55 MindMaps sans Frontières. D Kuryazov, F Schmalriede, C Schönberg, K Meyer and A Winter.

    Case paper

    Case paper


Break (14:30-15:00)


II. Afternoon block: Hands-on sessions (15:00-18:00)

We invite the participants to experiment with the collaborative modeling tools of their choice. Tool developers are invited to support the hands-on session assigned to their tools by providing a supervisory presence, and preferably a brief tutorial before the session starts. We encourage participants to experiment with multiple tools in the afternoon. We then ask the participants to evaluate their experience by completing an evaluation sheet. No prior knowledge is required for the hands-on session: the morning talks and the brief tutorials should provide sufficient information to enable working with the tools. The experiments will be conducted in Zoom breakout rooms.

Logistics

  • Zoom is used as the hosting video conference platform.
  • The main room serves as the lobby for entering the workshop as a visitor.
  • Upon entering, visitors can record their tool preferences and their availability in a Google Spreadsheet.
  • Upon meeting the requirements, a breakout room for a hands-on session will be created. The requirements are the following.
    • There are enough visitors/participants to start the session.
    • The visitors/participants are available for the entirety of the session.
    • There are enough resources to host the session, i.e., tool/VM, host person.
  • The above process is managed manually with reasonable automation if possible.
  • Observers might join the sessions, but cannot interact with the experiment.

Post-workshop publication

We are planning to report the valuable data collected during the workshop, along with the lessons learned, in a journal publication (e.g., SoSyM, STTT, or JOT) soon after the workshop. Interested participants will be invited to co-author this publication.
